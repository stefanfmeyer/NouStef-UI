# Beta Launch Plan

Goal: Launch a focused beta with clear owners, gating criteria, and a compact operating view that fits a Home workspace.

Context / assumptions:
- This is a planning-only deliverable.
- The goal is a reliable beta launch, not a broad public release.
- The team needs a compact workspace that shows status at a glance rather than a large PM system.
- No external research was requested, so dates are relative and can be finalized by the team.

Proposed approach:
- Organize the beta around one launch table plus a few compact supporting lists.
- Work backward from a target launch date across six tracks: product readiness, quality, operations, support, comms, and feedback.
- Use explicit launch gates so the beta only opens when critical readiness items are truly complete.

Compact Home workspace structure:
1. Launch overview
   - Beta goal
   - Target window
   - Launch owner
   - Current phase
   - Overall risk
2. Launch checklist table
   - Track, item, owner, due, status, blocker, notes
3. Launch gates
   - Must-pass criteria before opening beta
4. Top risks
   - Risk, impact, mitigation, owner
5. Beta metrics
   - Signups, activation, weekly active users, support volume, critical bugs
6. Feedback queue
   - Source, theme, severity, decision, owner

Launch gates:

| Gate | Pass condition | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Scope gate | Beta scope, exclusions, and success metrics documented | Product lead |
| Quality gate | No open P0s; P1 list reviewed with explicit waivers | Eng lead + QA |
| Observability gate | Dashboard and alerts live for core flows | Ops/Data |
| Support gate | Support channel, triage owner, and escalation policy active | Support lead |
| Safety gate | Rollback and incident playbook approved | Ops owner |
| Comms gate | Invite copy and expectation-setting materials approved | PMM |
| Decision gate | Go/no-go review completed with named approver | Launch owner |

Recommended beta metrics:

| Metric | Why it matters | Initial target / check |
|---|---|---|
| Invited users accepted | Measures invite quality and demand | Track by cohort |
| Activation rate | Measures whether users reach first value | Define one key activation event |
| Week-1 retention proxy | Measures early product pull | Check return usage or repeated key action |
| Critical error rate | Measures reliability | Must remain below agreed threshold |
| Support tickets per active user | Measures usability and operational load | Watch for spikes after launch |
| Time-to-first-response | Measures support quality | Keep within stated beta expectation |
| Top 5 feedback themes | Measures product learning | Review weekly |

Top risks and mitigations:

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| Scope creep before launch | Delays and unstable beta | Freeze scope at T-21 and push non-critical work out | Product lead |
| Missing telemetry | Hard to judge success or diagnose issues | Instrument and validate events before launch | Eng + Data |
| Too many users too soon | Support overload and noisy signal | Use invite cohorts and staged rollout | Launch owner |
| Unclear issue triage | Slow recovery and poor tester trust | Define severity, owner, SLA, escalation path | Support + Eng |
| No rollback discipline | Extended incident duration | Approve rollback triggers and operator checklist | Ops owner |

Suggested operating cadence:
- Weekly launch review from T-21 to T-7
- Daily standup from T-7 to T+3
- Formal go/no-go review at T-3
- Launch-day command channel with named incident lead
- Post-launch review at T+7 and T+14

Home workspace seed recommendation:
- Keep one primary table called Beta launch plan.
- Keep three compact support sections: Launch gates, Top risks, Beta metrics.
- Use a small summary header with owner, target date, phase, and overall risk.
- Prefer simple status values: Not started, In progress, Blocked, Done.

Validation / readiness checklist:
- Every launch-gated row has an owner and due date.
- Every Yes gate has a binary pass/fail decision before launch.
- Instrumentation events are tested in the live beta environment.
- Support and incident contacts are documented in one visible place.
- Success metrics are defined before the first invite goes out.

Open questions to resolve:
1. Who is the single beta launch owner?
2. What is the target beta date or launch window?
3. Which user cohort gets the first invite wave?
4. What counts as activation for this product?
5. What issue severity threshold blocks launch?
6. Is the beta closed/invite-only or self-serve with a waitlist?
