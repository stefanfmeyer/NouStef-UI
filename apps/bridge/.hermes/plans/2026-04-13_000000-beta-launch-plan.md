# Beta Launch Plan

Goal: Launch a controlled beta with a clear go/no-go process, reliable onboarding, measurable success criteria, and a compact operating cadence.

Current assumptions:
- Product is feature-complete enough for a limited external beta.
- Team can assign clear owners across product, engineering, support, and growth.
- Launch scope should favor reliability and user feedback over broad feature coverage.
- This plan is intentionally compact and suitable for a Home workspace summary view.

## Home workspace structure

Use these 5 compact sections/cards:
1. Launch scoreboard
2. Readiness checklist
3. Weekly timeline
4. Owners and workstreams
5. Risks and decisions

## 1) Launch scoreboard

| Area | Target for beta launch | Owner | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Beta cohort | 20-50 qualified users invited | Growth/Product | Planned |
| Activation | >= 60% complete core onboarding flow | Product | Planned |
| Reliability | >= 99% uptime during beta window | Engineering | Planned |
| Bug response | Critical bugs triaged within 2 hours | Engineering/Support | Planned |
| Feedback volume | >= 10 high-quality interviews or surveys in first 2 weeks | Product/Research | Planned |
| Retention signal | >= 30% of activated users return in week 2 | Product | Planned |
| Support burden | < 20% of users require manual intervention | Ops/Support | Planned |

## 2) Readiness checklist

| Track | Must be true before launch | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Product scope | Core use case works end-to-end without concierge intervention | Internal dogfood run + walkthrough recording |
| Onboarding | Signup, setup, and first-success moment are instrumented | Funnel dashboard and test account proof |
| Reliability | Error logging, alerting, retry/recovery paths, and rollback are live | Monitoring links + runbook |
| Support | Triage channel, FAQ, canned replies, and escalation owner exist | Support doc + owner list |
| Access control | Invite flow and cohort controls work reliably | Test invites sent successfully |
| Legal/privacy | Terms, privacy, and beta disclosure are reviewed | Approved docs |
| Feedback loop | In-app feedback, survey, and interview booking flow are live | Working links |
| Decisioning | Go/no-go criteria agreed in advance | Leadership sign-off |

## 3) Timeline

| Week | Theme | Key outputs |
|---|---|---|
| Week -3 | Scope freeze | Beta scope locked, known non-goals documented, launch KPIs agreed |
| Week -2 | Hardening | Fix top reliability issues, complete telemetry, create runbooks |
| Week -1 | Dry run | Internal beta rehearsal, seed support docs, verify invite + onboarding flows |
| Launch week | Controlled release | Invite first cohort, monitor activation/errors daily, hold daily launch standup |
| Week +1 | Stabilize | Patch critical issues, remove onboarding friction, gather structured feedback |
| Week +2 | Evaluate | Review KPIs, decide expand / hold / reset |

## 4) Owners and workstreams

| Workstream | Main owner | Responsibilities | Deliverables |
|---|---|---|---|
| Product | PM / Founder | Beta scope, success metrics, feedback synthesis | KPI sheet, cohort definition, go/no-go memo |
| Engineering | Eng lead | Reliability, telemetry, incident response, fixes | Monitoring, runbook, rollback plan, bug triage board |
| Design | Design lead | Onboarding clarity, friction reduction, feedback UX | Onboarding polish list, survey/in-app feedback UX |
| Growth | GTM / Growth | Recruitment, invite sequencing, comms | Invite list, email copy, waitlist rules |
| Support/Ops | Support lead | User help, escalation, FAQ, response SLAs | Support inbox/channel, macros, FAQ |

## 5) Risks and decisions

| Risk | Early warning sign | Mitigation | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| Users fail to reach first value | Low onboarding completion | Shorten setup, add guided defaults, manual assist for top accounts | Product |
| Reliability degrades under real usage | Error spikes, slow pages, failed jobs | Tight alerts, bounded retries, rollback switch, daily error review | Engineering |
| Beta cohort is low quality | Low engagement, noisy feedback | Tighten invite criteria and prioritize target personas | Growth/Product |
| Support load becomes manual and unsustainable | Repeated issues/tickets | Add FAQ, macros, known-issues page, fix top 3 causes fast | Support/Engineering |
| Team launches too broadly too early | Rising bugs before KPI confidence | Use staged rollout gates and explicit expansion criteria | Leadership |

## Go / no-go criteria

Launch only if all are true:
- No known Sev-1 issues on core flow
- Monitoring and alerts are active for critical paths
- Onboarding funnel is measurable end-to-end
- Support owner is on point during launch window
- Rollback path is tested
- At least 5 internal or friendly testers completed the core journey successfully

## Daily operating cadence during launch week

| Cadence | Participants | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 15-min launch standup | Product, engineering, support | Review incidents, blockers, invite pacing |
| Midday KPI check | Product + engineering | Activation, errors, drop-off points |
| End-of-day review | Core team | Decide fixes, comms, and next invite batch |

## Compact execution plan

1. Lock beta scope and define explicit non-goals.
2. Finalize success metrics: activation, reliability, retention, support burden, feedback volume.
3. Instrument the onboarding funnel and core success event.
4. Create launch runbook: alerts, escalation path, rollback steps, incident owner.
5. Run internal dogfood with 5-10 testers and log every onboarding failure.
6. Fix top onboarding and reliability blockers only; defer non-critical polish.
7. Prepare invite list and staged release batches.
8. Publish support FAQ and response macros.
9. Launch to first cohort and review KPIs daily.
10. At end of week 2, decide expand, hold, or reset.

## Open questions

- Who is the exact beta persona and what problem are they validating?
- What is the single most important activation event?
- How many manual interventions are acceptable during beta?
- What traffic or cohort size is safe for the first wave?
- What conditions trigger rollback or invite pause?

## Recommended first view in Home

If you want this to stay compact, the initial Home view should show only:
- Top row: beta status, cohort size, activation, reliability
- Middle: readiness checklist
- Bottom: timeline + top 3 risks

This keeps the workspace small, scannable, and operational.
